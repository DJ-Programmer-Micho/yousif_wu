I.Scope

This Policy applies to personal data we process about:

  • Senders, Receivers, and bill pay customers,
  • Users of our website/app and support channels,
  • Beneficial owners, authorized representatives, and other parties involved in a transaction,
  • Job applicants who apply through our website (if applicable).

This Policy does not cover third-party websites/services that may link to or from our Services.

II.What We Collect

A. Identification & KYC/AML Data

  • Full name, date/place of birth, nationality, gender.
  • Government ID data (number, type, issue/expiry, scan/photo), proof of address, tax IDs.
  • Biographic info, occupation/employment, source of funds/wealth, purpose of transaction, relationship to receiver.
  • Sanctions/PEP screening results (match/no-match metadata).

B. Contact & Account Data

  • Mobile numbers, email addresses, postal address.
  • Account credentials (hashed), language preferences, support history.

C. Transaction & Financial Data

  • Transaction details (amount, currency, fees, FX rate, TRN/MTCN, sender/receiver info).
  • Payment instrument details (masked card PAN, issuing country, IBAN/account numbers), bank name, wallet IDs.
  • Refunds/chargebacks and dispute notes.

D. Communications & Preferences

  • Copies of emails, chat/WhatsApp/Telegram/SMS records with our support.
  • Marketing consents and opt-out preferences.

E. Sensitive Data (limited)

  • Only where required by law/consent: ID photos, liveness/face matching metadata from verification vendors, adverse media flags, and sanctions/PEP indicators.

III. Sources of Data

  • Directly from you (onboarding, transfers, support, complaints).
  • Your device (via our apps/web, cookies/SDKs).
  • Third parties: banks, payout partners, payment processors, telecom/OTP providers, identity verification vendors, fraud-prevention and sanctions-screening providers, public and government databases, and, where lawful, marketing partners.

IV. How We Use Your Data (Purposes & Legal Bases)

We process personal data to:

  1. Provide the Services (open accounts; execute transfers; communicate statuses/receipts; customer care).
    Legal bases: contract performance; legitimate interests.
  2. KYC/AML/CFT & Sanctions Compliance (identity verification; screening against sanctions/PEP/adverse media; record-keeping; regulatory reporting; transaction monitoring; fraud prevention).
    Legal bases: legal obligations; substantial public interest (financial crime prevention); legitimate interests.
    Payment Processing (collect funds; process payouts/refunds; resolve chargebacks).
    Legal bases: contract performance; legitimate interests; legal obligations.
  3. Security & Abuse Prevention (protect accounts; investigate fraud; detect anomalies; secure our infrastructure).
    Legal bases: legitimate interests; legal obligations.
  4. Service Improvements & Analytics (quality control; performance analytics; product development).
    Legal bases: legitimate interests; consent where required (e.g., non-essential cookies).
  5. Marketing & Relationship Management (with your consent where required)—news, features, promotions.
    Legal bases: consent; legitimate interests (where permitted). You may opt out anytime.
  6. Legal & Regulatory (respond to lawful requests; enforce terms; defend legal claims).
    Legal bases: legal obligations; establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

We do not use personal data for automated decisions producing legal or similar significant effects without human oversight. Where we use automated risk/sanctions checks, a qualified reviewer can re-assess on request.

V. Sharing Your Data

We disclose personal data to:

  • Banks, payout partners, payment processors, and card schemes to execute transfers and payouts.
  • Identity verification, sanctions/PEP screening, and fraud-prevention providers (e.g., KYC vendors, watchlist match resolution).
  • Telecom/SMS/WhatsApp and email providers for OTPs and notifications.
  • IT/cloud hosting, data centers, and support platforms (secure infrastructure and tools).
  • Auditors, consultants, insurers, and professional advisers (where necessary).
  • Regulators and law enforcement where required or appropriate by law.
  • Corporate transactions (merger, acquisition, financing)—subject to confidentiality and applicable law.

We do not sell personal data. We do not allow our processors to use your data for their own marketing.

VI. International Transfers

We may transfer/process data outside your country (including outside the EEA/UK) on secure infrastructure. Where required, we implement appropriate safeguards (e.g., EU Standard Contractual Clauses, UK Addendum), risk assessments, and technical/organizational measures. You may request a copy of relevant safeguards at info@iraqremit.com (redactions may apply).

VII. Retention

We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes above, including:

  • KYC/AML records: typically 5–10 years after the end of the customer relationship or as required by local law/regulators.
  • Transaction records: per financial-record and AML obligations.
  • Marketing consents/preferences: until you withdraw or applicable limits expire.
  • Litigation holds: as long as reasonably necessary.

When no longer needed, data is securely deleted or anonymized.

X. Your Rights

Your rights depend on where you live. Subject to legal limits and AML/record-keeping duties, you may have rights to:

  • Access your data and obtain a copy,
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data,
  • Erase data (“right to be forgotten”) where applicable,
  • Restrict processing in certain cases,
  • Object to processing (including direct marketing),
  • Portability (receive data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format),
  • Withdraw consent where processing relies on consent (without affecting prior processing).

We may need to verify your identity and may be unable to delete data we must retain by law (e.g., KYC/AML). To exercise rights, contact info@iraqremit.com.

EEA/UK Residents. If GDPR/UK GDPR applies, our legal bases are described in Section 4. You may lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. We will cooperate with applicable authorities.
